Section 01

First two paragraphs carry the heaviest message load

Users decide quickly whether to continue reading. Keep opening lines clear, useful, and specific to reduce drop-off in text-heavy interfaces.

Structure supports scanning first, then deep reading. Clear hierarchy and spacing allow readers to choose where to focus without confusion.

Section 02

Design for scanners before readers

Use short sections, meaningful subheadings, and visible anchors along the left edge to maintain orientation throughout long content pages.

  • Start paragraphs with high-value keywords
  • Keep one key idea per paragraph
  • Use bullets to reduce scan effort

Section 03

Break monotony with visual rhythm

Alternating text density and media blocks keeps reading energy high while preserving the expected F scan behavior.

Use visuals as strategic pauses, not distractions. Every image block should support comprehension and flow.
